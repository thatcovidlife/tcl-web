## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are people who want to learn about the dangers of COVID and make better day-to-day decisions with that information. They come to the site to stay informed through curated news and scientific coverage, and to ask the chatbot practical questions in a trustworthy, accessible format.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel opinionated, modern, and scientific. The emotional target is a balance of calm, credibility, vigilance, hope, urgency, and care: serious enough to communicate risk clearly, but not cold, alarmist, or detached.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is news meets scientific education. The home page can borrow some of the editorial energy and hierarchy of The Next Web, with the authority and pacing of outlets like The Atlantic and Wired. The experience should not resemble a financial website: avoid finance-dashboard tropes, investor-style metric blocks, and market-terminal aesthetics. The product supports both light and dark modes and should treat both as first-class experiences. The existing primary red must remain a defining accent.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with editorial authority. Prioritize strong hierarchy, high-information layouts, and clear scanning over decorative UI patterns.
2. Make caution feel composed. Communicate seriousness and urgency without panic through disciplined typography, restrained motion, and careful contrast.
3. Keep the science legible. Present research, news, and chatbot guidance in a way that feels rigorous, current, and approachable rather than academic or sterile.
4. Use red with intent. Preserve the brand red as a sharp signal color, not a blanket wash across the interface.
5. Avoid finance aesthetics. No trading-dashboard cues, synthetic metric theater, or visuals that suggest markets, speculation, or performance tracking.

### Working Assumptions
No special accessibility target was specified beyond standard strong practice, so future work should maintain readable contrast, keyboard accessibility, and reduced-motion support by default.
